## Abstract

Generative models, such as Diffusion and Flow Matching, have emerged as the dominant paradigm for robotic visuomotor policies. However, their reliance on multi-step iterative solvers introduces prohibitive inference latency, severely hindering high-frequency closed-loop control. While recent single-step acceleration methods attempt to force these curved action manifolds into straight trajectories, they inevitably suffer from catastrophic spatial truncation errors, low-pass filtering effects (loss of high-frequency details), and mode averaging in multimodal scenarios. To overcome these inherent bottlenecks, we propose a novel single-step generative framework. First, we introduce **Recursive Consistent Action Flow (RCAF)**, which leverages a high-order recursive estimation to compute an explicit residual heading correction, fundamentally neutralizing spatial truncation errors. Second, to recover delicate manipulation precision, we propose **Dual-Timestep Frequency Consistency (DTFC)**, which employs an adaptive spectral constraint to naturally enforce a coarse-to-fine optimization paradigm. Finally, a **Contrastive Flow Matching (CFM)** mechanism is incorporated to decouple interwoven multimodal vector fields, ensuring collision-free phase space trajectories. Extensive experiments across 2D and 3D bimanual/dexterous manipulation benchmarks (RoboTwin, Adroit, DexArt), as well as real-world hardware deployments, demonstrate our framework's superiority. Utilizing only a single forward pass (1 NFE), our method not only significantly outperforms 10-step state-of-the-art baselines but also eclipses massive Vision-Language-Action (VLA) foundation models, unlocking high-fidelity, ultra-fast robotic control.
